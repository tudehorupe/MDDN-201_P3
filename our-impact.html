our impact

Everything we do has an impact.

From what we eat & drink, how we travel, and how we consume culture, various activities have impacts on the environment. In order to mitigate this issue, first an understanding of the various forms our impact on the natural environment has is important to begin this process of change.

air pollution

What is it?

Air pollution is the release of pollutants into the air that are detrimental to human health and the plant as a whole.

What causes it?

Mainly coming from energy use and production, such as burning fossil fuels, a release of gases and chemicals enter the air damaging the quality of it for various species. In the form of carbon dioxide and methane, this then leads to higher disease risks and rising temperatures. Not only is air pollution a destructive force, it has also increased the rate at which the earth’s temperature is changing.

Why it matters?

This then leads to higher disease risks and rising temperatures, as in 2016, this resulted in 4.2 million premature deaths. Not only is air pollution a destructive force, it has also increased the rate at which the earth’s temperature is changing, therefore, causing it to be a considerable global hazard.

Deforestation

What is it?

Deforestation is the process of clearing, destroying, or otherwise removing trees for various activities.

What causes it?

Farming, grazing of livestock, mining, and drilling together equate to more than half of all deforestation. This is due to an increased demand for different supplies in which extra land is required in order to produce this content.

Why it matters?

Not only does deforestation affect local people and animals immediately involved with the location, it also has significant impacts for the wider world. Given the process of trees absorbing CO2 from the atmosphere and producing oxygen, the retention of large forest masses is crucial for a healthy plant, and therefore a healthy lifestyle for wildlife and humans.
